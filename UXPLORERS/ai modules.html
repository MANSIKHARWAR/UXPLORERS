Government Initiatives for Neurodiverse Women in India

1. Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities (DEPwD)

The DEPwD, under the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, is pivotal in implementing policies and schemes for persons with disabilities, including neurodiverse women. Key initiatives include:

  • Sugamya Bharat Abhiyan: Aims to create an accessible and inclusive environment for persons with disabilities, enhancing their participation in society.
  • National Helpline for Divyangjan (14456): Provides 24x7 assistance to persons with disabilities, offering information and support services.

For more details, visit the official website: DEPwD

2. National Trust for Welfare of Persons with Autism, Cerebral Palsy, Mental Retardation, and Multiple Disabilities

This autonomous body offers several schemes aimed at supporting neurodiverse individuals, including women:

  • Samarth Scheme: Provides residential care facilities for individuals with disabilities, ensuring a safe and supportive environment.
  • Gharaunda Scheme: Offers affordable housing and lifelong care services for adults with autism, promoting independent living.
  • Niramaya Health Insurance Scheme: Provides affordable medical care, covering therapies and corrective surgeries for persons with disabilities.

Explore more at: The National Trust

3. Unique Disability ID (UDID)

The UDID project aims to create a national database for persons with disabilities, issuing a Unique Disability Identity Card. This card simplifies access to various government schemes and ensures uniformity across the country.

4. Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment

The Ministry oversees various schemes and programs for persons with disabilities, including:

  • Assistance to Disabled Persons for Purchase/Fitting of Aids and Appliances (ADIP Scheme): Provides financial assistance for purchasing or fitting aids and appliances.
  • Deendayal Disabled Rehabilitation Scheme (DDRS): Promotes voluntary action for persons with disabilities, supporting NGOs and institutions working in the sector.
  • National Awards and Scholarships: Recognizes outstanding achievements and provides financial support for education.

Visit: Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment

5. Nari Shakti Puraskar

This prestigious award recognizes exceptional women for their contributions to society. While not exclusively for neurodiverse women, it highlights the achievements of women across various fields, including those with disabilities.

6. Accessible India Campaign

Launched to enhance accessibility in public spaces and transport systems, this initiative aims to create a barrier-free environment for persons with disabilities, fostering their inclusion in all aspects of life.

7. State-Level Initiatives

Various state governments have implemented programs to support neurodiverse women, including:

  • Skill Development Programs: Offering vocational training and employment opportunities.
  • Awareness Campaigns: Educating the public about neurodiversity and promoting inclusivity.
  • Support Services: Providing counseling and legal assistance.

For state-specific information, refer to the respective state's Department of Social Welfare or Disabilities.
